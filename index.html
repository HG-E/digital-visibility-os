The problem

Excellent research. Hidden from the world.

Researchers at well-resourced institutions have communications teams, PR support, and infrastructure. Most researchers don't. DVOS closes that gap.

🔍

Name disambiguation

Your publications are scattered across databases under different name spellings. Citations aren't attributed correctly. Funders can't find your full record.

DVOS links your ORCID to consolidate everything under one verified identity.

📊

Invisible impact

Your h-index might be excellent for your field and career stage — but you have no way to know how you compare to peers globally or regionally.

DVOS provides field-normalized citation benchmarks (Hirsch 2005, Bornmann & Marx 2011).

💰

Missed funding

Grant opportunities from Wellcome, NIH Fogarty, Gates Foundation, and African research councils exist — but most researchers never find them.

DVOS surfaces matched grant opportunities based on your field and career stage.

🤝

Collaboration isolation

A researcher in Nigeria and a researcher in Kenya may be working on the same topic — and never discover each other. No system exists to bridge them.

DVOS shows active researchers in your field ranked by recent citation impact.

✍️

Writing inequality

Grant applications and LinkedIn profiles written by non-native English speakers are evaluated on language, not science. That's not fair.

DVOS generates professional bios, LinkedIn summaries, and grant impact statements from your research data.

🏛️

No research website

Most researchers have no personal website. A Google search for your name returns nothing — or worse, the wrong person.

DVOS builds a professional research website from your profile data in one click.

FAQ

Common questions

DVOS (Digital Visibility OS) is a free tool for academic researchers. It helps you build and track your research presence online — covering citations, ORCID, publications, website, content, and grants. It's designed for researchers who don't have institutional communications support, particularly in the Global South.
DVOS is completely free and requires no signup. All citation tracking, ORCID verification, publication linking, score computation, and the research intelligence feed are free. AI features (bio generation, grant intelligence, content engine) require an AI proxy connection — you can set one up with ~$5 of Anthropic credits. No subscription, no recurring cost.
DVOS computes a score from 0–100 using six weighted signals: ORCID completeness (25%), citation impact (27%), researcher profile (18%), research website (12%), content output (10%), and platform presence (8%). Citation impact uses a logarithmic scale to reward improvement at every level — not just for highly-cited researchers. The score reflects how discoverable your work is to collaborators, funders, and journals.
ORCID is a free 16-digit persistent identifier for researchers — like a passport number for your academic work. It solves name disambiguation (two researchers named "Emmanuel Okafor"), ensures publications are credited correctly, and is required for most international grant applications (EU Horizon, Wellcome Trust, NIH). ORCID counts for 25% of your visibility score — the single largest signal.
No. DVOS is a client-side application — all data is stored in your browser's localStorage and never sent to any DVOS server. When you fetch citation data from OpenAlex or ORCID, those requests go directly from your browser to those public APIs. Your research profile exists only on your device.
DVOS pulls from four open academic databases: OpenAlex (250M+ works, 90M+ authors), Semantic Scholar (AI-enhanced citations), Crossref (DOI registry), and PubMed (biomedical literature). It uses the highest citation count across all four sources, and detects when you have publications in PubMed or Crossref that aren't yet in OpenAlex.
Yes. The Research Pulse feature includes an AI-matched grant intelligence feed (refreshed monthly) and a conference radar (refreshed weekly). Grants are matched to your field, institution location, and career stage — prioritizing open programs from Wellcome Trust, NIH Fogarty, Gates Foundation, African Union, NRF South Africa, TDR, EDCTP, and field-specific foundations. Conferences include region and prestige filters. All AI-generated data includes a disclaimer to verify official websites before submitting.
