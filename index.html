Common questions.

DVOS (Digital Visibility OS) is a free tool that helps researchers build their online academic presence. It connects your ORCID profile, tracks your citations in real time, generates a professional researcher bio, and keeps you updated on conferences and grant opportunities in your field.
Yes. DVOS is completely free — no signup, no subscription, no credit card. Open it, enter your name, and get your visibility score in under two minutes. AI features (bio generation, grant intelligence) require an optional AI proxy setup, but all live data and scoring are free.
DVOS queries OpenAlex, Semantic Scholar, Crossref, and PubMed simultaneously — the four largest open research databases. It cross-references results, takes the highest verified count, and shows your total citations, h-index, and how you compare to peers at your career stage and field.
DVOS computes a score from 0–100 across six signals: ORCID completeness (25%), citation impact (27%), researcher profile (18%), research website (12%), content output (10%), and platform presence (8%). The score reflects how discoverable your work is to collaborators, funders, and journals.
Yes — DVOS was specifically designed for researchers in the Global South. It prioritizes free, openly accessible tools, surfaces grants from Wellcome Trust, NIH Fogarty, Gates Foundation, African Union, and NRF South Africa, and shows conferences with known fee-waiver programs. No institutional subscription required.
ORCID is a free, persistent identifier for researchers — like a passport for your academic work. It solves the name disambiguation problem (two researchers with the same name), ensures all your publications are attributed to you across databases, and is required for most international grant applications including EU Horizon and Wellcome Trust.
